Things to Do in Malaysia

Explore the culture, history, and beauty of Malaysia.

Notable Cultural Sites

  • Petronas Twin Towers - Iconic skyscrapers in Kuala Lumpur.
  • Batu Caves - A limestone hill with a series of caves and temples.
  • George Town, Penang - A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its colonial architecture and street art.
  • Malacca City - A historic city with Dutch, Portuguese, and British influences.
  • Mount Kinabalu - The highest peak in Southeast Asia, located in Sabah.

Malaysian Culture and Art Forms

Malaysia is a melting pot of cultures, with Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous influences shaping its unique identity. Here are some highlights:

  • Traditional Dance - Experience the grace of Malay dances like Joget and Zapin, or the vibrant Indian Bharatanatyam and Chinese Lion Dance.
  • Music - Listen to traditional instruments like the gamelan and rebana, or enjoy modern Malaysian pop and indie music.
  • Crafts - Discover intricate batik textiles, songket weaving, and traditional woodcarving.
  • Festivals - Celebrate cultural events like Hari Raya, Chinese New Year, Deepavali, and Harvest Festival (Pesta Kaamatan).

Bako National Park

Located in Sarawak, Bako National Park is one of Malaysia's oldest and most diverse national parks. It offers:

  • Wildlife - Spot proboscis monkeys, bearded pigs, and a variety of bird species.
  • Hiking Trails - Explore trails ranging from easy walks to challenging hikes, with stunning coastal and rainforest views.
  • Beaches - Relax on secluded beaches like Teluk Pandan Kecil.
  • Unique Landscapes - Marvel at sea stacks, cliffs, and mangrove forests.

Bako National Park is a must-visit for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
